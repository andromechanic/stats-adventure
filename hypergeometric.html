Sampling without Replacement from a Finite Group

01

The Hook

"If I draw 5 cards from a deck of 52, what's the chance I get 2 Aces?"

Unlike the Binomial distribution, which assumes each trial is independent (like flipping a coin), the Hypergeometric distribution models what happens when you don't put the ball back in the jar.

Key Difference

"Since you aren't replacing what you take, the probability of success changes as you draw more items."
