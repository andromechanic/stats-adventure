The Hook

From human heights to test scores, the Universe loves the Normal Distribution.

Also known as the Gaussian Distribution, this curve appears whenever independent random variables are added together. It is completely defined by two parameters: its Mean (\(\mu\)) and its Std Dev (\(\sigma\)).

The Mathematical Power

\( f(x) = \frac{1}{\sigma\sqrt{2\pi}} e^{-\frac{1}{2}(\frac{x-\mu}{\sigma})^2} \)

Gauss

The Z-Score Transformation

Any Normal Distribution can be mapped to a Standard Normal (\(\mu=0, \sigma=1\)) using:

\( Z = \frac{x - \mu}{\sigma} \)

Central Limit Theorem

"The sum of enough random variables will always look normal, no matter what their original distribution was."
