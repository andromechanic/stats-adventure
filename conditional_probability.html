Calculating Likelihood Based on Known Evidence. This is the bridge to Bayes' Theorem.

01

The Core Concept

Conditional probability measures the probability of an event occurring, given that another event (the condition) is already known to have occurred.

The fundamental logic is Restricting the Sample Space to only the outcomes that satisfy the condition.

02

Mathematical Notation

\( P(A | B) \)

|

"Given That"

Read as: "The probability of A happening, assuming B is true."

04

How the Fraction is Calculated

Example: $P(D | \neg T)$

Probability of Dandruff ($D$), given No Treatment ($\neg T$):

1

The Universe (Denominator): Look only at the "No Treatment" row. The total number of people in this row is 8.

2

The Target (Numerator): Within that row of 8 people, find how many have Dandruff ($D$). The table shows 5.

3
\( \frac{5}{8} = 0.625 \)

Note: This same "Restricted Universe" logic applies to every permutation below.

05. All Conditional Permutations

Perspective A: What's the outcome given treatment status?

Dandruff given Treatment:

\( P(D | T) = \frac{0}{2} = \mathbf{0} \)

No Dandruff given Treatment:

\( P(\neg D | T) = \frac{2}{2} = \mathbf{1.0} \)

Dandruff given No Treatment:

\( P(D | \neg T) = \frac{5}{8} = \mathbf{0.625} \)

No Dandruff given No Treatment:

\( P(\neg D | \neg T) = \frac{3}{8} = \mathbf{0.375} \)

Perspective B: What was the treatment given the outcome?

Treatment given Dandruff:

\( P(T | D) = \frac{0}{5} = \mathbf{0} \)

No Treatment given Dandruff:

\( P(\neg T | D) = \frac{5}{5} = \mathbf{1.0} \)

Treatment given No Dandruff:

\( P(T | \neg D) = \frac{2}{5} = \mathbf{0.4} \)

No Treatment given No Dandruff:

\( P(\neg T | \neg D) = \frac{3}{5} = \mathbf{0.6} \)
