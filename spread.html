Quantifying Variance and Diversity in Your Data

01

The Range

The simplest measure: The difference between the maximum and minimum values.

\( Range = Max - Min \)

Primary Weakness

"Entirely dependent on Outliers. Doesn't tell us how the majority of data is distributed."

02

Interquartile Range (IQR)

The spread of the Middle 50% of the data. This is the gold standard for Skewed datasets because it ignores the extreme 25% at both ends.

\( IQR = Q_3 - Q_1 \)

03

Standard Deviation (\(\sigma\))

Measuring the average distance of each data point from the Mean.

\( \sigma = \sqrt{\frac{\sum(x - \mu)^2}{n}} \)

Distance Squared Average Root

SD

Intuition Check

A Low \(\sigma\) means data is clustered tightly around the mean (Consistent). A High \(\sigma\) means data is spread out (Volatile).

04

Variance (\(\sigma^2\))

The average of the squared differences from the mean. It is Standard Deviation before the square root.

\( \sigma^2 = \frac{\sum(x - \mu)^2}{n} \)

"Why square it? To remove negative signs and punish large deviations more heavily."

05

Mean Absolute Deviation

The average of the absolute distances from the mean.

\( MAD = \frac{\sum |x - \mu|}{n} \)

Unlike Variance, MAD doesn't "punish" outliers by squaring them, making it more intuitive but less mathematically flexible.

06

Coefficient of Variation (CV)

A relative measure of spread, expressed as a percentage of the mean.

\( CV = \left( \frac{\sigma}{\mu} \right) \times 100\% \)

The Killer Feature

Allows you to compare the spread of datasets with different units (e.g. Height in cm vs Weight in kg).

07

The Empirical Rule

In a Normal Distribution, standard deviations predict the coverage:

68%
Of all data falls within 1 Standard Deviation (\(\pm 1\sigma\)).
95%
Of all data falls within 2 Standard Deviations (\(\pm 2\sigma\)).
99.7%
Of all data falls within 3 Standard Deviations (\(\pm 3\sigma\)).

08. The Statistical Verdict

Which spread should you report?

Skewed Data

"Report the IQR. It protects your summary from being distorted by weird outliers."

Normal Data

"Report the Standard Deviation. It provides a complete mathematical profile."
