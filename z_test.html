The Z-Test

Measuring the Distance of Means in Standard Units

01

The Hook

"How many standard deviations away is my result from the population average?"

The Z-Test is our primary way of comparing the mean of a sample to a known population mean (\(\mu\)). It assumes you have a large sample size (\(n > 30\)) and that your data follows the Normal Distribution.

\( Z = \frac{\bar{x} - \mu}{\sigma/\sqrt{n}} \)

The "Danger Zone"

95% Confidence

For a 2-tailed test, the critical Z values are ±1.96.

The Verdict

If your Z score is larger than 1.96, your result is statistically significant at the 5% level.
