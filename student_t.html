Small Sample Inference

Student's
T-Distribution

Inference with Smaller Datasets and Fat Tails

01

The Hook

"What if I only have 10 data points and don't know the population standard deviation?"

The Z-score assumes you know everything about the population. But in the real world, we only have small samples. The T-distribution is "cautious"—it has Fatter Tails to account for the uncertainty of small sample sizes.

History Byte

"Invented by William Gosset under the pseudonym 'Student' while working for Guinness Brewery."

02

Degrees of Freedom (\(df\))

The "Sample Size" parameter that controls the curve's width.

\( df = n - 1 \)

SAMPLE

Low \(df\)

Fatter tails, shorter peak. Higher chance of extreme events.

High \(df\)

Approaches the Standard Normal (\(Z\)) distribution.
