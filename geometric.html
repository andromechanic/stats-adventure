Probability of the First Success after $k$ Failures

01

The Hook

"How many times must I roll a die until I get a 6?"

The Geometric distribution model the number of Bernoulli trials needed to get the very first success. It is "memoryless"—each failure doesn't make the next success any more likely.

Memoryless Property

"If you've already failed 10 times, the chance you succeed on the 11th is exactly the same as the chance you had on the 1st."

When to Use?

Ideal for quality control (testing items until one fails) or modeling how long a customer waits before taking an action. It always has its peak at the very first trial and decays exponentially.
