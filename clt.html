03

The Core Identities

Mean of Means (\(\mu_{\bar{x}}\))

\( \mu_{\bar{x}} = \mu \)

The average of your sample means is exactly the same as the original population mean.

Standard Error (\(\sigma_{\bar{x}}\))

\( \sigma_{\bar{x}} = \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}} \)

As your sample size grows, the uncertainty (spread) of your mean shrinks by the square root of $n$.

04. The Logical Verdict

Why it works

High values in one sample tend to be balanced by low values in another. The larger the sample, the higher the probability that these "extremes" cancel out, leaving you at the True Population Center.
