The Logic of Decision

Hypothesis
Testing

The courtroom of statistics—where ideas are on trial and evidence must be "beyond reasonable doubt."

01

The Legal Strategy

"You are innocent until proven guilty."

In statistics, we never try to "prove" our idea is right. Instead, we assume the boring thing is true, and then check if our data is so weird that it makes the "boring thing" impossible.

Null Hypothesis (\(H_0\))

"Nothing is happening."

The status quo. No effect, no change, no difference. This is what we are trying to reject.

Alternative (\(H_a\))

"Something IS happening."

Our claim. There is an effect, a change, or a significant difference.

02

The 4-Step Gauntlet

01

State the Hypotheses

Define your \(H_0\) and \(H_a\) clearly.

02

Choose Significance ($\alpha$)

Usually 0.05. This is your "Bar of Proof."

03

Calculate Test Statistic

Find the Z, T, or Chi-Square value.

04

Compare P-Value

If P-Value < \(\alpha\), you win (Reject \(H_0\)).
