01

The Hook

"How can I turn thousands of data points into a single decision?"

A Test Statistic is a numerical summary that represents the distance between your sample data and the Null Hypothesis. It measures "discrepancy." The bigger the number, the more surprised we are.

General Formula:

\( \text{Stat} = \frac{\text{Observed} - \text{Null}}{\text{Standard Error}} \)

03

The "Free to Vary" Concept

Most test statistics ($t, F, \chi^2$) come with Degrees of Freedom ($df$). Think of these as the constraints on your data. They adjust the "hurdle" you have to jump over to prove your result is significant.

"The more complex your model (more groups, more variables), the more $df$ you 'spend' to estimate parameters."
