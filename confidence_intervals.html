The Net vs The Spear

"Point estimation is like trying to hit a fish with a spear. Interval estimation is like using a net."

Rather than giving a single number (Sample Mean), a Confidence Interval gives us a range. It tells us how much precision we really have given the sample size ($n$) and the variation ($\sigma$).

\( CI = \bar{x} \pm Z^* \left(\frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}\right) \)

03. The Confidence Verdict

What 95% means

It doesn't mean your specific range has a 95% chance of being right. It means if we repeated this and built 100 nets, exactly 95 of them would successfully catch the fish.
