What is a
P-Value?

Quantifying the "Surprise" Factor of Your Data

01

The Evidence Meter

"If the Null Hypothesis (\(H_0\)) were true, how likely is it that we would see a result this extreme?"

A p-value is not the probability that the hypothesis is true. It is a measure of compatibility between your data and the Null Hypothesis. A low p-value means your data is very "surprising" if the world were truly random.

Low P-Value

"Wait, that's weird!"

Strong evidence to Reject the Null.

High P-Value

"Nothing to see here."

Data is perfectly normal under the Null.

03

The Conflict

H₀

Null Hypothesis

"The medicine does nothing. The difference you see is just luck."

Hₐ

Alternative Hypothesis

"The medicine works. The difference is too big to be a coincidence."

04. The Statistical Verdict

\( p < 0.05 \)

"Statistically Significant. We have enough evidence to say something interesting is happening."

\( p \ge 0.05 \)

"Not Statistically Significant. We fail to reject the status quo. More data might be needed."
