01

The Hook

"Rolling a fair die, spinning a wheel, or picking a truly random number."

The Uniform distribution represents complete randomness within a fixed range. There are no "preferred" values—the probability density is a flat line spanning from point \(a\) to point \(b\).

Discrete

Choosing from a list of fixed values (e.g. {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6}).

Continuous

Choosing from an infinite range of decimals (e.g. [0.0, 1.0]).

04. The Statistical Verdict

Information Paradox

A Uniform distribution represents Maximum Entropy when we know the bounds but nothing else. It is the "default" belief in Bayesian statistics (the Uninformative Prior).
