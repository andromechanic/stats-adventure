Predicting Success over Repeated Independent Trials

01

The Hook

"If I flip a coin 10 times, what's the chance I get exactly 7 heads?"

The Binomial distribution models the number of successes in fixed number of independent trials where each trial has the same probability of success.

  • Fixed Trials ($n$): You decide the number of attempts beforehand.
  • Fixed Probability ($p$): The chance of success doesn't change.
  • Independence: One trial doesn't affect the next.
02

The Core Logic

\( P(X=k) = \binom{n}{k} p^k (1-p)^{n-k} \)
