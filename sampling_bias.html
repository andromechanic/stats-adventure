01

The Hook

"A big sample is not always a good sample."

Imagine you want to know the average income of a city, so you stand outside a luxury car dealership and ask people. Your mean will be massive—but it will be totally wrong. This is Selection Bias.

Selection Bias

Certain groups are systematically excluded from the results.

Survival Bias

Only looking at the "winners" or "survivors" and ignoring the failures that aren't anymore.

03. The Logical Verdict

The Golden Rule

The only defense against bias is **Randomness**. A truly random sample ensures that every single perspective or data point has an equal chance of being heard.

Randomness is the Antidote to Assumption.
