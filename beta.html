01

The Hook

"What do I think the conversion rate of this website is if 10 people clicked out of 100?"

The Beta distribution is unique because it is defined on the interval \([0, 1]\). This makes it perfect for representing uncertainty about a probability. It is the conjugate prior for Bernoulli and Binomial processes in Bayesian statistics.

\( f(x; \alpha, \beta) = \frac{x^{\alpha-1} (1-x)^{\beta-1}}{B(\alpha, \beta)} \)

"Prior + Data = Posterior"

02

The Tuning Knobs (\(\alpha, \beta\))

The shape of the Beta distribution is incredibly flexible.

U-Shaped
Bell-Curve
Flat (Uniform)

Think of it this way:

\(\alpha\) = Successful outcomes + 1, \(\beta\) = Failed outcomes + 1.
