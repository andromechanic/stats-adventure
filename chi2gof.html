Evaluating if Observed Data Matches a Hypothesized Distribution

01

The Big Idea

The Goal

Do your observed counts "fit" what you expected to see? This test compares the distribution of a single categorical variable against a known or theoretical distribution.

\( H_0 \): The Fit is Good

"The observed data follows the expected distribution."

\( H_a \): The Fit is Poor

"The observed data significantly deviates from expectations."

02

Observed Frequencies ($O$)

Example: Customer Preference for 4 Smartphone Colors (n = 200)

Color Option Observed Count ($O$) Hypothesized %
Midnight Black 65 25% (Equal)
Starlight White 42 25% (Equal)
Deep Purple 58 25% (Equal)
Ocean Blue 35 25% (Equal)
Total Sample 200 (n) 100%
03

Expected Frequencies ($E$)

$$ E_i = n \times p_i $$

Where $n$ is total sample and $p_i$ is hypothesized probability

The Uniform Assumption

If we expect Equal Preferences, every category should ideally have the same count.

Calculation for each color

\( 200 \times 0.25 = \mathbf{50} \)

04

The \(\chi^2\) Calculation

$$ \chi^2 = \sum \frac{(O - E)^2}{E} $$
