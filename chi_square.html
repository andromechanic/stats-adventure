01

The Hook

"How can I tell if my categorical data matches my expectations?"

The Chi-Square (\(\chi^2\)) distribution is a continuous distribution that arises when you square and sum independent Standard Normal variables. It's the engine behind tests of independence and goodness-of-fit.

\( \chi^2 = \sum Z_i^2 \)

SQUARED

02

The Role of \(k\)

Like the T-distribution, \(\chi^2\) changes shape based on Degrees of Freedom (\(k\)).

Low \(k\) (\(k \le 2\))

Highly skewed, starting at infinity or peaking near zero.

High \(k\)

Smooths out and eventually mimics a Normal Distribution.
